Out of the inkwell

Out of the Inkwell was released in 1918 by Max Fleischer of Fleischer studios. It starred Koko the clown and Max Flerischer himself and would begin with Fleischer drawing Koko onto a page of paper where the clown would promptly come to life. Something of note that I just like that is only vaugely related is that Fleischer studiors pioneered rotoscoping, the technology that allowed animators to draw over live action footage to get more fluid life like movements. Koko the clown was rotoscoped off of Dave Fleischer in a clown suit. For the time, out of the inkwell was revolutionary
